Quadratic formula
{{Infobox mathematics}}
The '''quadratic formula''' describes the solutions of a quadratic equation:
<math>x = \frac{-b \pm \sqrt{b^2 - 4ac}}{2a}</math>

== Derivation ==
Completing the square yields:
<math>ax^2 + bx + c = a\left(x + \frac{b}{2a}\right)^2 + \left(c - \frac{b^2}{4a}\right)</math>

== Discriminant ==
The discriminant is <math>\Delta = b^2 - 4ac</math>.
